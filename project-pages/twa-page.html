The Wizard's Apprentice
Action-Adventure Souls-like where you play as an alchemist wizard in a school of gifted apprentices, but you’re not one of them.
Company: Barking Beagle Studios
Platforms: PC
Team Size: 5
Project Lenght: ~10 months
Key Responsibilities: Quest, Combat and Gameplay Designer and Programmer
Engine and Tools: Unity, Rider IDE, GitHub, Notion, Canva,Google Sheets and Docs
Project Details / Background

I began working on this project alongside my co-founder at Barking Beagle Studios, Maria Gomes. This eventually became the first project developed at our studio. Over the course of development, three artists joined our team, expanding both our scope and creative direction. The project took approximately 10 months to complete: 2 months of prototyping, 3 months of design, and 5 months dedicated to developing a vertical slice.

The game is set in a medieval fantasy world where the player takes on the role of a wizard’s apprentice at a prestigious school for gifted students, yet they are not one of them. Driven by unhealthy ambition, the protagonist discovers a forbidden method of casting magic through alchemy. From that point onward, the player is faced with a defining choice: continue their studies and accept their limitations, or embrace their ambition and unlock a new level of power at the cost of their humanity.

The core gameplay loop revolves around exploration, completing quests, defeating enemies, and collecting resources used to craft increasingly powerful spells.

The project initially began as a 2D top-down action-adventure game. However, as more artists joined the team, we transitioned to a third-person, souls-like action-adventure format, as it better aligned with our evolving design goals.

Summary Responsibilities

As Game Designer in this project, I was responsible for designing the Quest, Combat and Gameplay systems.

Quest design focused on reinforcing the fantasy of being a wizard’s apprentice, balancing imposed duties with player-driven exploration. The system is structured into three types: errands (repeatable resource and combat tasks), side quests (narrative-driven content with unique rewards), and main quests (multi-step objectives that drive progression and story).

In the vertical slice, I designed 2 quests. A side quest where the player investigates the existence and location of a mythical creature. Defeating it rewards the player with a key component required to craft a more powerful spell than those available at the start. And a small errand quest, where the player is tasked by the quest giver to collect some resources that will help them create a spell that will facilitate the fight with the creature the Player is seeking, reinforcing progression through exploration and combat.

The enemy featured in this quest was also designed by me, drawing inspiration from Portuguese mythology. Its design and mechanics revolve around themes of fire and hanging, which are reflected across its entire moveset. It features a single-target fireball, an area-of-effect fire explosion centered on itself, and a rope-based grab attack that pulls the player toward the enemy. To maintain pressure, the enemy also includes gap-closing abilities such as a dash and a leap, encouraging constant player movement and awareness.

Exploration complements progression by allowing players to freely navigate the world while naturally gating difficulty through enemy strength and environmental hazards, encouraging preparation and gradual mastery.

Combat is built around a hybrid spellcasting system combining basic spells (always available but low impact) and consumable alchemical spells crafted from gathered resources. This creates a loop of exploration, combat, and preparation, where players must plan their loadout and adapt to encounters.

Spells are categorized into multiple types and tied to elemental interactions, while enemies feature corresponding strengths and weaknesses, reinforcing strategic decision-making. The system draws inspiration from Fable and The Witcher 3, combined with the deliberate, skill-based combat of the Dark Souls series.

As the Game Programmer on this project, I was responsible for implementing core gameplay systems, including player movement and camera controls, as well as enemy AI behavior. I developed responsive and fluid character movement to support both exploration and combat, alongside a dynamic third-person camera system that ensured clarity and control during encounters. On the AI side, I designed and implemented enemy behaviors, including combat actions, state management, and navigation, with a focus on creating engaging and challenging interactions for the player.
