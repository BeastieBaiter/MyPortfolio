The Wizard's Apprentice
Action-Adventure Souls-like where you play as an alchemist wizard in a school of gifted apprentices, but you’re not one of them.
Project Details / Background
Company: Barking Beagle Studios
Platforms: PC
Team Size: 5
Project Lenght: ~10 months
Key Responsibilities: Quest, Combat and Gameplay Designer and Programmer
Engine and Tools: Unity, Rider IDE, GitHub, Notion, Canva,Google Sheets and Docs
Summary Responsibilities

As both Game Designer and Programmer on The Wizard's Apprentice, I owned the design and implementation of the Quest, Combat, and core Gameplay systems across the project's ten-month development cycle.

Quest design was built around reinforcing the fantasy of being a wizard's apprentice, balancing imposed duties with player-driven exploration and discovery. The system is structured around three quest types: errands (repeatable resource and combat tasks that keep the world feeling alive), side quests (narrative-driven content with unique rewards that expand the world's lore), and main quests (multi-step objectives that drive story progression and gate major abilities).

For the vertical slice, I designed two quests. The first is a side quest in which the player investigates the existence and location of a mythical creature. Defeating it rewards the player with a rare component needed to craft a significantly more powerful spell than anything available at the start of the game, creating a clear, earned sense of progression. Supporting it is a short errand quest, in which an NPC tasks the player with gathering resources used to craft a spell that aids in the coming fight, reinforcing the loop of exploration, preparation, and combat before a major encounter.

The enemy featured in this questline was also designed by me, drawing inspiration from Portuguese mythology. Its visual identity and combat moveset are built around themes of fire and hanging, reflected consistently across every ability. Its toolkit includes a single-target fireball, an area-of-effect fire explosion centred on itself, and a rope-based grab attack that pulls the player into melee range. To maintain pressure and punish passive play, the enemy also has gap-closing tools — a dash and a leap — that demand constant movement and spatial awareness from the player.

Exploration is designed to complement progression organically. The world is freely navigable from the start, but difficulty is naturally gated through enemy strength and environmental hazards, encouraging players to prepare thoroughly and develop mastery gradually rather than rushing objectives.

Combat is built around a hybrid spellcasting system combining basic spells — always available but limited in impact — with consumable alchemical spells crafted from resources gathered during exploration. This creates a satisfying loop of exploration, preparation, and combat, where players must think ahead about their loadout and adapt their approach to each encounter. Spells are categorised by type and tied to elemental interactions, while enemies carry corresponding strengths and weaknesses, rewarding strategic thinking and experimentation. The system draws direct inspiration from the tonal freedom of Fable, the systemic depth of The Witcher 3, and the deliberate, skill-based tension of the Dark Souls series.

On the programming side, I was responsible for implementing the core gameplay systems that bring the design to life. This included building responsive and fluid character movement and a dynamic third-person camera system designed to maintain clarity and player control during both exploration and combat encounters. I also designed and implemented the enemy AI — including combat actions, state management, and navigation — with a focus on creating enemies that feel reactive, threatening, and fair to fight.
