HPV: Combate Viral
AA card battler game where the player will be fighting the HPV virus against misinformation and infection, with the goal of raising awareness and educating teenage high-school students about HPV virus while providing a fun and educational experience.
Project Details / Background
Company: GameDev Técnico and LPCC - Portuguese League on the Fight Against Cancer
Platforms: PC, Android and Web
Team Size: 6
Project Lenght: ~5 months
Key Responsibilities: Project Lead, Combat and Gameplay Designer and Lead Programmer
Engine and Tools: Unity, Rider IDE, GitHub, Notion, Google Sheets, Slides and Docs
Game link: PlayStore
Summary Responsibilities

HPV: Combate Viral was developed in partnership between GameDev Técnico and the Portuguese League on the Fight Against Cancer (LPCC), with the goal of raising awareness and educating teenage high-school students about the HPV virus through an engaging card battler experience. I served as Project Lead, Lead Designer, and Lead Programmer across the project's five-month development cycle, leading a team of five.

As Project Lead and Producer, I was responsible for the full production pipeline, defining milestones, managing the team's workload, coordinating communication with the LPCC stakeholders, and ensuring the project shipped on time across PC, Android, and Web. Balancing the educational requirements set by our partner with the need to deliver a genuinely fun and engaging player experience was one of the core challenges of the project, and one I navigated closely with both the design and client teams throughout development.

On the design side, I led the combat and gameplay design of the card battler system, crafting mechanics that were accessible to a teenage audience while embedding HPV-related educational content naturally into the gameplay loop. From card abilities tied to immune system responses to enemy archetypes representing misinformation and viral infection.

View the full Game Design Document

As Lead Programmer, I oversaw the technical implementation of the game's core systems and was the sole developer responsible for the entire UI, from architecture and state management through to every animation in the game, all implemented using DOTween. This included the main menu, card interaction feedback, combat flow transitions, tutorial overlays, and result screens. Owning the full UI layer end-to-end meant ensuring that every interaction felt responsive, polished, and clear. Particularly important given the game's young target audience. You can see a demonstration of the UI work below.

Shipping a live product across three platforms, including Android via the Play Store and a web build. Within a five-month timeline with a small volunteer team was a significant production challenge, and one I am proud to have delivered successfully alongside a talented group of collaborators.
