Buried Within Clay
Puzzle-adventure game where you’re trapped with your archeological expedition deep within ancient Sumerian ruins. Labyrinthine puzzles, deadly traps, and something festers below the surface. Will you escape? And who will you sacrifice?
Project Details / Background
Company: Barking Beagle Studios
Platforms: PC
Team Size: 5
Project Lenght: ~10 months
Key Responsibilities: Movement, Physics, Camera and Systems Programmer
Engine and Tools: Unity, Visual Studio Code, GitHub, ClickUp, YouTrack, Google Sheets and Docs
Summary Responsibilities

As the Movement, Physics, Camera and Systems Programmer on Buried Within Clay, I was responsible for the feel and reliability of everything the player directly experiences through their controller. This included building and tuning the character movement system, physics interactions, and camera behaviour to match the tense, atmospheric tone of the game.

A key technical highlight of my work on this project was the PlayerBorders system — a runtime component that dynamically positions a ring of invisible collider walls around the player character using raycasting. Rather than relying on manually placed geometry to prevent the player from falling off ledges or getting stuck in unintended areas, the system automatically detects ledge boundaries in all directions by firing downward raycasts at configurable test intervals. Each border collider is then precisely repositioned frame-by-frame at the detected ledge edge, accounting for the character controller's radius and skin width to ensure a seamless, invisible barrier.

Player Borders Demo GIF.

This removed a significant content production bottleneck: designers and artists could build large, open, visually complex spaces without needing to hand-place invisible walls around every ledge — the system handled it automatically. It also included built-in Editor debug tooling, with toggleable colour-coded raycast visualisation to assist during development and QA.
